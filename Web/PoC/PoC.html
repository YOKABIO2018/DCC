Page Overview

        In this page, we simulate the entire system to show the validity of our idea. The ATP controller of the DNA circuit, introduced on the System Design page, worked and simulated a series of aspects affecting biological reactions. In this study, we targeted the Ras activation mechanism and simulated whether to suppress Ras activation by ATP Controller. Proof of Concept

Overall Simulation

        For the evaluation method of the simulation, we use integral as the evaluation item in three of amplification, duration, integral shown in reference [x]. Next, the scheme of the transmission system used in this simulation is shown.(Figure x)


Hereinafter, let $\beta$[-] be the ATP consumption efficiency of the ATP controller. Since this can not be confirmed in this project, we simulated with various values. First, we show the simulation results of the part surrounded by the red line in the whole drawing of this project(Figure2).


The number of chemical species, parameters, rate equations, and differential equations used in the simulation are shown in Table 1 below.[x][x]


Time change of activated Ras (part surrounded by the blue line of Figure x) when Threshold [nM] is constant and $\beta$ is changed is shown in Figure 3 and its integral value is shown below as Figure 4.

From Figs. 3 and 4, it can be seen that Ras activation is suppressed as the value of $\beta$ is increased, that is, as the consumption efficiency of ATP is increased. Also, compared with the case where $\beta$ = 0, that is, when the circuit that consumes ATP is not operated (normal Kholodenko model), it can be confirmed that activation of Ras is suppressed in either case. Next, time change of activated Ras (part surrounded by blue line of Figure x) when $\beta$ is constant and Threshold is varied is shown in Figure 5 and its integral value is shown below as Figure 6. For the role of Threshold, see DNA Circuit of System Design.

As shown in Figures 5 and 6, increasing the value of Threshold, that is, the longer the ATP controller turns on, the more ATP is consumed, so it can be seen that Ras activation is suppressed. Also in this case, as in the previous case, compared with the case where ATP Controller was not operated (normal Kholodenko model), in any case, it can be confirmed that activation of Ras is suppressed. From these results, the following can be confirmed(Figure 7).

From Figure 9, it can be confirmed that the larger the Threshold value, the more the amount of generation of Absorber (ON), that is, the longer the ON state of the ATP controller is.

ATP Absorption

        Next, we simulate ATP Controller to consume ATP(Figure 10).


The reaction in which Absorber(ON) absorbs ATP is described by the following rate equation of $v_{1} \sim v_{5}$.


$v_{1} = -\alpha_{1} \cdot [\rm R_{2}] \cdot [\rm ATP],\ \alpha_{1}=3.0×10^{-4}[\rm 1/nMs]$
$v_{2} = -\alpha_{2} \cdot [\rm R$-$\rm PL] \cdot [\rm ATP],\ \alpha_{2}=7.0×10^{-4}[\rm 1/nMs]$
$v_{3} = -\alpha_{3} \cdot [\rm R$-$\rm Sh] \cdot [\rm ATP],\ \alpha_{3}=9.0×10^{-4}[\rm 1/nMs]$
$v_{4} = \gamma,\ \gamma=90.0[\rm nM/s]$
$v_{5} = -\beta \cdot [\rm Absorber(ON)] \cdot [\rm ATP]$

Here, $v_{1} \sim v_{3}$ represent the rate equations in the following scheme drawing(Figure 11). In addition, it is assumed that $v_{4}$ represents the production rate of ATP generated in the cell, and this time it was assumed to be generated at a constant generation rate. The values of $\alpha_{1} \sim \alpha_{3}$ and $\gamma$ of the coefficient were determined while comparing it with the result of [x]. $v_{5}$ shows how Absorber(ON) generated by ATP Controller consumes ATP. As mentioned at the beginning, $\beta$ is a coefficient representing the consumption efficiency of ATP by ATP Controller.


Using the rate equation of $v_{1} \sim v_{5}$, the differential equation of ATP is as follows.

$\large \frac{d[\rm ATP]}{dt} = v_{1} + v_{2} + v_{3} + v_{4} + v_{5}$

The manner in which ATP is absorbed and decreased by Absorber(ON) generated by ATP Controller is shown below(Figure 12,13). Figure 12 shows the temporal change of ATP concentration with respect to the value of $\beta$ when Threshold is constant. Figure 13 shows the temporal change of the concentration of ATP with respect to the value of Threshold when $\beta$ is constant. In either case, the initial concentration of ATP is 10000[nM].

From Figures 12 and 13, in both cases, it can be confirmed that the concentration of ATP decreases when B = 0, that is, when compared with the ATP of the ordinary Kholodenko model. Further, from Figure 12, it can be seen that ATP decreases as the value of $\beta$ increases, that is, as ATP consumption efficiency increases. Furthermore, it can be seen that ATP decreases as the Threshold value increases, that is, as the DNA circuit turns ON for a longer time.

Inhibit Activation of Ras

        Finally, we simulate the change of Ras activation due to the reduction of ATP. The part where ATP is involved in the activation of Ras is the part shown in Figure 14 below.


The reactions ① to ③ in Figure 14 are represented by the following formulas.

① $k_{3} \cdot [{\rm R_{2}}]-k_{-3} \cdot [{\rm RP}],\ k_{3}=1{\rm [1/s]},\ k_{-3}=0.01{\rm [1/s]}$
② $k_{6} \cdot [{\rm R}$-${\rm PL}]-k_{-6} \cdot [{\rm R}$-${\rm PLP}],\ k_{6}=1{\rm [1/s]},\ k_{-6}=0.05{\rm [1/s]}$
③ $k_{14} \cdot [{\rm R}$-${\rm Sh}]-k_{-14} \cdot [{\rm R}$-${\rm ShP}],\ k_{14}=6{\rm [1/s]},\ k_{-14}=0.06{\rm [1/s]}$


For this time, it is assumed that these reaction rate coefficients $k_{x},k_{-x}$ change linearly with the concentration of ATP. Also assume that the concentration of ATP is kept constant, and the reaction rate coefficients at time $t$ are $k_{x}^{'},k_{-x}^{'}$ and are determined as follows.

$\large k_{x}^{'}=\frac{[{\rm ATP}](t)}{{\rm [ATP]}(0)} \cdot k_{x},\ k_{-x}^{'}=\frac{{[\rm ATP]}(t)}{{\rm [ATP]}(0)} \cdot k_{-x}$


$In addition, $v_{6} \sim v_{8} represent the reactions involved in Ras activation and represent 26, 27, 28 in the following scheme diagram(Figure 15). Ras activated by Ras-GTP is represented by Ras before activation of Ras-GDP.


$\large v_{6} = \frac{k_{26} \cdot [\rm R‐Sh‐G‐S] \cdot [\rm Ras‐GDP]}{k_{-26}+[\rm Ras‐GDP]}$

$\large v_{7} = \frac{k_{27} \cdot [\rm R‐G‐S] \cdot [\rm Ras‐GDP]}{k_{-27}+[\rm Ras‐GDP]}$

$\large v_{8} = \frac{k_{28} \cdot [\rm Ras‐GTP]}{k_{-28}+[\rm Ras‐GTP]}$

However,

$ k_{26}=5.40×10^{1}{\rm [1/s]},\ k_{-26}=2.28×10^{2}{\rm [nM]},$

$\ k_{27}=5.40×10^{1}{\rm [1/s]},\ k_{-27}=2.28×10^{2}{\rm [nM]},$

$\ k_{28}=3.09×10^{1}{\rm [1/s]},\ k_{-28}=2.76×10^{1}{\rm [nM]}$


The values of these coefficients were referred to [(x)]. $v_{6},v_{7}$ represent Ras activation (phosphorylation), $v_{8}$ represents inactivation (dephosphorylation) of Ras. Therefore, the differential equations of Ras-GTP and Ras-GDP are as follows.

$\large \frac{d[\rm Ras‐GTP]}{dt} = v_{6}+v_{7}-v_{8}$

$\large \frac{d[\rm Ras‐GDP]}{dt} = -v_{6}-v_{7}+v_{8}$

Therefore, when simulation is carried out using the above equation and the reaction rate coefficients described above, Figure 2 to 5 as described at the beginning are obtained. Also, as a result of simulating the case of $\beta=0,\ 5.0×10^{-6},\ 5.0×10^{-5},\ 5.0×10^{-4}$, and Threshold$=5,\ 50,\ 95$, the integrated value of the concentration of activated Ras is as shown in Figure 16. From this result, it can be confirmed that Ras activation can be suppressed by adjusting $\beta$ and Threshold.
